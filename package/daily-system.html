A short method guide

The Daily Log Method

Five small principles for getting more out of Daily Log — and out of your days. Read it once; it takes five minutes.

Daily Log doesn't nag, gamify, or ship your data to the cloud. That means the habit has to come from you, not from notifications. Here's the small system that makes it stick.

Principle 01

Keep few habits, and keep them visible

Three or four habits you actually do beats ten you feel guilty about. The point of a habit tracker isn't the tracking — it's the daily glance that reminds you who you're trying to be.

Try

Start with three habits. Only add a fourth once the first three feel automatic.

Principle 02

Plan the day on the board, not in your head

Every morning, drop the day's real work into To do. Move one thing to Doing at a time. The board is a promise you make to yourself in the morning and keep by evening.

Try

Cap “Doing” at one card. Finish it before you start another.

Principle 03

Connect the daily to the distant

Big goals fail quietly because no single day feels urgent. In Daily Log, link a daily task to a long-term goal and watch the progress bar move. Small work, made visible, compounds.

Try

Pick one long-term goal. Link at least one task to it every day this week.

Principle 04

Review the week; adjust the next

Open the Review tab each Sunday. Which habits held? Where did the tasks pile up? Write one honest line in the week note — not a scorecard, a course-correction.

Try

End every week with one sentence: “Next week I'll ___.”

Principle 05

Miss a day. Never miss two.

Streaks are motivating until they break — then they can make you quit. Treat one missed day as noise. The only rule that matters: don't miss twice in a row. That's how a streak survives being human.

Try

Missed yesterday? Do the smallest possible version today. Showing up counts.
